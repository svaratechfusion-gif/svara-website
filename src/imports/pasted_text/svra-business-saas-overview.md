MARKET CONTEXT & OPPORTUNITY

Over the last two decades, Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) has transformed how organisations operate—digitising core business functions such as sales, operations, finance, human resources, and customer service.

However, as SaaS adoption has matured, a new set of challenges has emerged:

Enterprises rely on multiple disconnected tools across functions
Data is fragmented across systems, limiting visibility and insight
Workflows require manual coordination between platforms
AI capabilities are embedded in isolated tools rather than across the organisation
Total cost of ownership increases with each additional system

This has resulted in a paradox: while organisations have more software than ever, they operate with less cohesion and limited system-level intelligence.

At the same time, the rise of AI is redefining enterprise software. The next generation of business platforms will not be defined by features alone, but by their ability to:

Integrate seamlessly across functions
Operate on shared data and intelligence layers
Automate workflows across the organisation
Provide predictive insights and decision support

This marks the transition from traditional SaaS to AI-native business systems.

The opportunity lies in building platforms that are not just tools, but operational backbones for intelligent enterprises.
