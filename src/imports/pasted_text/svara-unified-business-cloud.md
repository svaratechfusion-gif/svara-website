MARKET CONTEXT & OPPORTUNITY

Over the past decade, enterprises have adopted a wide range of SaaS products to manage core business functions—CRM for sales, ERP for operations, HRMS for workforce management, LMS for training, and support systems for customer service.

While these tools have improved functional efficiency, they have also introduced structural challenges:

Fragmentation of data across multiple platforms
Lack of interoperability between systems
Redundant workflows and manual data transfer
Inconsistent insights due to siloed information
Increased cost and complexity of managing multiple vendors

As organisations scale, this fragmentation becomes a major bottleneck. Decision-making slows down, operational visibility decreases, and efficiency gains from individual tools are offset by system-level inefficiencies.

At the same time, AI is being introduced into individual SaaS products, creating isolated pockets of intelligence rather than a unified system.

The next evolution of enterprise software is not more tools—it is fewer, more integrated platforms with shared intelligence across all business functions.

This is the shift from SaaS to AI-native business systems.
